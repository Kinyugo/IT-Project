IT and the Future

Which technology did you choose?

The technology chosen is 3D printing.

Who is this technology designed for?

3D printing finds many uses from health care, art & design, industrial manufacturing to security.

Choose one of the domains of interest. Explain Why you have an interest in this domain?

The chosen domain of interest in health care. This is because 3D printing has the most positive impact in this domain. It could be used to quickly prototype prosthetics, medicine as well as medical equipment which could mean massive saving of human lives as well as cheap and accessible health care.

What is the purpose of your chosen computing innovation? What does it help to accomplish? What are the intended positive impacts of the technology?

3D printing involves construction of a 3-dimensional object from a digital model through the use of computer controlled methods.

  1. Design Freedom. 3D printing enables designers and manufacturers to create complex designs that otherwise would be cumbersome to create using traditional methods.
  2. Quick prototyping. Use of 3D printing especially combined with AI enables designers to quickly iterate through their designs in a shorter time span than was previously possible.
  3. Reduced waste production. 3D printing only uses the material needed for the process, unlike traditional methods where materials have to be cut into required sizes leading to production of a lot of waste.
  4. Improved design to production speed. 3D printing achieves what would take human effort days in a matter of hours reducing the time to production for a design. This is beneficial as it allows manufacturers to quickly test their products in the real world and make adjustments.
  5. Reduced production costs. 3D printing eliminates multiple manufacturing stages of the traditional manufacturing pipelines, this means reduced human labor cost as well as machinery and space cost.

How can this technology be misused? What might be some unintended negative impacts? You might consider impacts on society, the economy, culture, etc.

Some cons of 3D printing are:

  1. Huge initial cost. 3D printers are very expensive, thus small companies without huge income may be unable to meet the cost of buying one. This may lead to them growing at a slower rate compared to their bigger counterparts.
  2. Uncontrolled design freedom. While design freedom can be a good thing, uncontrolled design freedom may lead to substandard goods being produced thus endangering human lives. Also, this makes it easier for terrorists to manufacture their own weapons with unprecedented destruction ability
  3. Material limitations. Materials that can be used with 3D printing are limited this means that people who want to take advantage of 3D printing are forced to use only the available materials; metal and plastic while traditional manufacturing methods may permit use of cheaper materials.
  4. Small build volumes. Most 3D printers have a small build chamber and thus may prevent creation of large items.
